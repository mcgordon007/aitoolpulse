## What Is Perplexity Comet?

Perplexity Comet is an AI-native web browser built from the ground up — not a Chrome extension or a chatbot bolted onto an existing browser, but a completely new kind of browsing experience. Launched in July 2025 and updated significantly in March 2026 with Skills, Model Council, Voice Mode, and GPT-5.3-Codex integration, Comet represents Perplexity's vision for what the browser of the AI era should look like.

The core concept is "agentic browsing" — instead of you clicking and navigating the web yourself, Comet Assistant can do it for you. Think of it as having a second pair of hands inside your browser, one that can read pages, click links, open tabs, fill forms, and execute multi-step tasks on your behalf.

*Version verified: Comet launched July 9, 2025 (TechCrunch); March 13, 2026 update confirmed via Perplexity Changelog. Sources: TechCrunch + Perplexity Changelog (2026-05-20).*

## Key Features of Perplexity Comet

### 1. Comet Assistant — Your In-Browser AI Agent

The star of the show is Comet Assistant, a built-in AI agent that lives inside every tab. You can open a sidecar panel on any web page, and Comet will see exactly what's on that page and answer questions about it — or take actions.

Real examples from users:

- "Summarize all the reviews of this hotel across the 12 tabs I have open"
- "Find me the cheapest nonstop flight from NYC to Tokyo next Tuesday"
- "Go through this product page and compare it to the three competitors I have open"

### 2. Agentic Task Execution

Comet doesn't just find information — it can act on it. Based on March 2026 updates, Comet now supports:

- **Skills:** Pre-built automation workflows (e.g., "book a restaurant", "compile research on X topic")
- **Model Council:** Chooses the best underlying model for each task dynamically
- **Voice Mode:** Hands-free voice commands for browser control
- **GPT-5.3-Codex coding integration:** For complex research tasks that involve data gathering and synthesis

### 3. Contextual Understanding Across Tabs

Unlike a normal browser where each tab is isolated, Comet Assistant can see all your open tabs and use that context. If you're comparing five products across five tabs, you can ask Comet to summarize the price/feature differences across all of them at once.

### 4. Deep Research Mode

Perplexity's Deep Research, upgraded in early 2026, is integrated into Comet. Ask it to research a topic, and it will: review dozens of sources, refine its research plan, develop a comprehensive report in 2-4 minutes, and let you download it as a PDF or share it.

## Real-World Use Cases That Actually Work

Based on user reviews and hands-on testing from March-May 2026:

### ✅ Shopping & Price Comparison

Comet can browse multiple e-commerce sites, compare prices, and even complete a purchase — all without you touching the keyboard. Best for: consumer electronics, travel bookings, insurance quotes.

### ✅ Research & Competitive Analysis

Feed Comet a competitor name and watch it open 10 tabs, read each site, and return a structured analysis. Best for: market research, job seeking, academic literature reviews.

### ✅ Travel Planning

"Find me a boutique hotel in Barcelona for July 15-18 under $180/night with a rating above 4.5 and breakfast included." Comet will search, filter, and present short-listed options — then book if you confirm.

### ✅ Content Monitoring

Set Comet to watch specific websites or product pages and alert you when conditions change (price drops, new reviews, inventory updates).

## ⚠️ Security Concerns: The CometJacking Vulnerability

No article about Comet would be complete without addressing the elephant in the room. In October 2024, security firm LayerX identified a vulnerability dubbed "CometJacking" — a prompt-injection exploit that could potentially allow malicious web pages to manipulate Comet's AI assistant through crafted content.

Perplexity has acknowledged the issue and stated they're working on mitigations. The practical risk to average users remains low, but it's worth being thoughtful about:

- Not letting Comet autofill sensitive forms on untrusted sites
- Reviewing permissions before granting access
- Keeping an eye on what your assistant is doing (you can always take control)

This isn't unique to Comet — the same class of vulnerability affects all agentic browsers including Google's Chrome AI features. But it's real and worth knowing about.

*Source: LayerX Security report (Oct 2024) + LinkedIn analysis by Oscar Le (2026).*

## Is Comet Ready for Mainstream Use?

After 30 days of hands-on testing (per Towards AI's March 2026 review), the verdict is nuanced:

**For power users and researchers:** Yes — Comet saves meaningful time on multi-tab research and complex search tasks

**For casual browsing:** Probably not yet — the agentic features add complexity that isn't needed for simple tasks

**For enterprise:** Growing adoption; Comet enterprise rollout began in early 2026

The browser fundamentally alters the web experience. Whether that's a net positive depends heavily on your workflow — but for those who live in tabs and hate repetitive clicking, Comet is one of the most genuinely useful AI tools to emerge in 2025-2026.
