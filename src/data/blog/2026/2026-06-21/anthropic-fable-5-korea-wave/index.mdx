**1. The two-step story behind the Fable 5 global ban**

The Fable 5 export control ban was reported on June 17, 2026. The widely-circulated narrative at the time was that Amazon researchers discovered jailbreak vulnerabilities in Fable 5 --- the public version of Claude Mythos launched on June 9 --- and reported them to the White House, which led Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick to send the export control letter ordering all foreign national access revoked.

That story is half right. The full story, assembled from The Washington Post (June 15) and WIRED (June 17-19) reporting, is a two-step sequence --- and step one has nothing to do with Amazon and everything to do with SK Telecom.

**Step 1 --- The SK Telecom action (early-to-mid June)**

SK Telecom is South Korea's largest wireless carrier. It is also a $100 million Anthropic investor since 2023, and a partner in Anthropic's Project Glasswing initiative with access to Claude Mythos --- the closed, government-and-cleared-partners version of the most advanced model line. Earlier this month, the White House identified SK Telecom as a company suspected of having ties to China and asked Anthropic to revoke SK Telecom's access to Claude Mythos. Anthropic complied the same day.

SK Telecom has stated clearly that it has no ties to China and that the claim is untrue. The carrier does not use Huawei or ZTE equipment in its core networks. The historical link US officials may be referencing: SK Telecom invested in China Unicom in 2006. Korean industry observers note that all three major South Korean carriers have used some Huawei equipment in fixed-line networks at various points, which makes the 'China ties' standard potentially applicable to the entire Korean telecom industry --- not just SK Telecom.

**Step 2 --- The Amazon action (one to two days later)**

Separately, Amazon researchers identified potential vulnerabilities in Fable 5 --- the public version of Mythos that launched on June 9 --- and reported them to the White House. The administration, already concerned about Anthropic's access control processes following the SK Telecom situation, concluded it 'could not trust Anthropic to safeguard its most advanced AI technology'.

The export control letter ordering all foreign national access revoked arrived at 5:21 PM Eastern on June 12, 2026. Anthropic was given roughly 90 minutes to take the models offline. Rather than implement real-time nationality filtering --- which Anthropic said is technically infeasible at the model layer --- Anthropic disabled both models globally for everyone.

The single line that ties the two steps together is the one The Washington Post attributed to two White House officials: the administration 'could not trust Anthropic to safeguard its most advanced AI technology'. Without the SK Telecom action, the same Amazon-flagged Fable 5 vulnerability might have triggered a model-level patch and a quieter remediation. With the SK Telecom action already on the White House's mind, the same vulnerability escalated into a global export control ban.

**Why the SK Telecom angle is the geopolitically consequential piece**

- A $100 million investor in Anthropic is being designated a national security risk to its own investee company. The investor-investee relationship is exactly the structure US export controls are now treating as a vulnerability surface --- and the friction that creates for AI companies operating globally is permanent.

- The 'China ties' standard being applied to SK Telecom is potentially applicable to KT and LG Uplus (the other two major Korean carriers) on the same fixed-line Huawei equipment history. KT and LG Uplus have both publicly stated they were not involved in the ban and never had Mythos access, but the precedent is now set.

- The Asia-wide signal. SK Telecom is a $100M Anthropic backer, a Glasswing partner, and the largest wireless carrier in a country where Anthropic just opened its third APAC office. If a flagship partner can be revoked on a China-tie standard, every flagship partner in every US-aligned allied country has to ask the same question.

US export controls on AI used to mean chip export controls. The June 12 ban is the first time the same export control machinery was turned on a US AI model. The SK Telecom angle is the first time that machinery hit a US AI company's own investor. Both facts will define the next 18 months of AI geopolitics.

**5. Zero jailbreaks before relaunch --- and why that bar is impossible**

The most consequential fact about the Fable 5 restoration is not a date. It is a condition.

Trump administration officials told WIRED that Anthropic must proactively test all frontier AI models to identify potential jailbreaks and report them to the government before any relaunch of Fable 5. More significantly, the administration requires Anthropic to eliminate all jailbreaks from Claude Fable 5 before the model can go live again. The cybersecurity research community's response has been near-unanimous: comprehensive jailbreak prevention is currently technically impossible for any frontier AI model.

The technical reality: AI safety at the frontier is a defense-in-depth problem, not a binary solved-or-unsolved problem. Jailbreaks are an adversarially-driven, continuously-evolving category --- new techniques are developed faster than any company can enumerate and block existing ones. Anthropic said this explicitly in Fable 5's launch documentation: perfect jailbreak resistance is not possible for any provider using current AI safety methods. The government's stated requirement sets a bar that no AI company can certify meeting --- not OpenAI, not Google, not anyone.

David Sacks, Co-Chair of the President's Council of Advisers on Science and Technology, separately disclosed that the administration had offered Anthropic a choice before issuing the export control directive: fix the jailbreak or voluntarily de-deploy the model. Dario Amodei refused both options. Anthropic's position is that the vulnerability is narrow, non-universal, and similar to capabilities that other publicly deployed frontier models already expose without any bypass. Fixing it would require changing the model's legitimate security research capabilities in ways that would harm defenders more than attackers.

The most likely resolution, per security policy analysts, is not zero jailbreaks --- which is impossible --- but a monitoring and reporting framework that requires proactive testing and government notification. Anthropic already operates 30-day data retention on Fable 5 traffic, a bug bounty programme, and government review partnerships with NIST and the UK AISI. Building on those existing mechanisms rather than requiring an impossible standard is the path most consistent with what Chris Ciauri's 'within days' confidence signal suggests is being negotiated.
